The fear in trying to live a complete life

<-- Back to the main page

Written on the 26th of March, 2026

What is the purpose of life? This is quite a hard question to answer, with it being even harder to find a direct answer to it. Something that fascinates me is that although this question persists as an ambiguous sentiment in everyone, the will to live a complete life remains in our soul until the end.

But what does it mean to live a complete life? Unfortunately, I fell into the trap of obsessing over this question. The truth is that I will probably never know, and if I do, it will be when I’m too old to actually live it. This is where the fear comes in. How can you be certain that what you are doing isn’t in vain? How can you reassure yourself that you won’t regret what you are doing?

I went through a phase where I was preoccupied with those ideas in every single activity that I took part in. I was so anxious to “live” that I ended up not living at all.

These thoughts will never leave my mind, and I think to a certain extent it’s beneficial for me to have a force to keep me grounded. However, I learnt to find beauty throughout all this chaos. I think that you wouldn’t want the film or series you are watching to be spoiled. You should feel the same about your life. I suppose that if someone tells me what I should do to feel complete, I will end up feeling like a preprogrammed prisoner without any personal autonomy at all.

I don’t have an answer to anything that I mentioned, however, no matter what, I am glad that I can try to live my life how I see best. We are so lucky to have the opportunity to do whatever we please, even if it means regretting it afterwards. Yes, I might be living my life horribly wrong, and yes, I might feel immense regret afterwards, but at least I had the chance to feel something.
